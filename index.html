FAQ

Delphos soundworlds are generators that are trained and controlled by their copyright owners exclusively. Soundworld owners are paid for every transaction involving their soundworld and are able to control how the soundworld is used. If the soundworld is shared with other consumers, this is done solely at the direction of the soundworld owner and is retractable at any time.

Delphos Music takes a very strong position regarding copyright ownership and supporting musicians, no matter what legislation regarding so-called 'fair use' may exist.

Each transaction via Delphos is counted in credits. Generating a loop may cost 1 credit, generating a stem pack will cost several credits.

The credit system also allows soundworld creators to set their own pricing on their soundworlds when sharing them with other users across the Delphos network.

We only support instrumental music for the time being. This is because there are increased legal complexities with reproducing human voices and likeness. In order to protect our clients and users, we currently do not support vocal synthesis.

If you own the rights to the music you use to train your soundworld, then you own the output compositions.
